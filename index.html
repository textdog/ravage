Comedy of manners stage show of byzantine customer service calls and unrelated footage as background. This is an outside-the-head assessment of reality dictated by call scripts. Their angry eloquence showcases the mental and emotional disarray of working class America.

Synopsis
This experimental narrative focuses on what Mark Twain referred to as "a time so remote, so far back toward the beginning of original idiocy", that is, NOW, and when you can't tell a contractor from a tennis ball.

(e.g. I'm a performance artist, look at me! They don't floss, they'd rather be laid off.)

Characters
A working-class American finds herself embroiled as a contractor for telecom call centers in the face of a small town war.

Mo and Gouche are suburban discards who find themselves in mental and emotional disarray because the phone rings.
